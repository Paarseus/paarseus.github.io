Field Testing & Validation

Parsa Ghasemi1, Behnam Bahr1
1Cal Poly Pomona
Part of DRIVE Platform
AVROS

Systematic field testing with GPS trail mapping, telemetry analysis, and safety validation across 50+ hours of autonomous operation.

Abstract

We present the field testing and validation methodology for a full-scale autonomous vehicle platform. The system has been validated over 50+ hours of autonomous operation with zero safety incidents. A comprehensive data acquisition pipeline logs 2,581 telemetry rows at 20 Hz with dual-camera synchronization at 30 FPS. GPS trail mapping with 784 waypoints enables comparison of planned versus actual vehicle trajectories. The four-layer E-STOP architecture achieves sub-200ms emergency response time across all operating conditions.

GPS Trail Mapping

GPS trail mapping captures 784 waypoints during autonomous operation, visualized using Folium on campus map tiles. Comparison of planned routes with actual GPS traces reveals path-following accuracy and identifies areas where the navigation system deviates from intended trajectories. This data drives iterative improvements to the Pure Pursuit controller's lookahead parameters and the DWA planner's cost functions.

Telemetry Analysis

The data acquisition system logs 2,581 telemetry rows at 20 Hz, capturing vehicle speed, steering angle, throttle position, brake state, GPS coordinates, IMU orientation, and system health metrics. Dual-camera synchronization at 30 FPS provides time-aligned visual records of every test run. Post-test analysis of telemetry data identifies control system performance characteristics and informs parameter tuning.

Safety Validation

The four-layer E-STOP architecture has maintained zero safety incidents across 50+ hours of autonomous testing. Safety validation includes measurement of emergency response times (sub-200ms from trigger to full stop), watchdog timeout behavior verification, and systematic testing of failure modes across the distributed CAN network. Every test session is logged with safety metrics for ongoing risk assessment.
