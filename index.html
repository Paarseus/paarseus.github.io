End-to-End Autonomous Vehicle Research Platform

Converting a utility vehicle into a fully autonomous research platform for $12K-15K lets ~30 undergraduates build and debug every layer of the stack—from LiDAR occupancy grids and RTK-GPS localization to Nav2 path planning—at a fraction of what commercial alternatives cost.

Distributed Embedded Vehicle Control System

Four Teensy 4.1 MCUs on a CAN bus run closed-loop steering, throttle, and brake control in 4,900 lines of C++. A four-layer E-STOP and hardware watchdog have kept it at zero safety incidents through 50+ hours of autonomous driving, all on a custom PCB I designed in KiCad.

project page / av-control-system / AVPCB

Real-Time Vehicle Teleoperation & WebUI with Voice Control

Drive the vehicle from a simchair, a phone, or just your voice—a WebSocket bridge streams commands to the CAN master while WebRTC pipes back live video. Whisper runs on-device for speech-to-text control, and priority arbitration keeps everything safe when multiple inputs compete.
