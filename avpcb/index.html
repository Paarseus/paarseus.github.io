Custom Vehicle Control PCB

Parsa Ghasemi1
1Cal Poly Pomona
AVPCB

A custom PCB integrating CAN transceivers, level shifters, and DC-DC converters for the autonomous vehicle's distributed control system.

Abstract

We present a custom printed circuit board designed in KiCad for the autonomous vehicle's distributed embedded control system. The board integrates SN65HVD230 CAN transceivers for reliable differential signaling on the 250 kbps CAN bus, TXB0104D bidirectional level shifters for mixed-voltage logic interfacing between 3.3V and 5V domains, and Traco DC-DC converters for efficient power regulation from the vehicle's 12V battery. The PCB provides clean power distribution, sensor connector breakouts, and a compact form factor designed for mounting in the vehicle's electronics bay.

Schematic Design

The schematic design centers on three key subsystems: CAN bus communication using SN65HVD230 transceivers with proper termination and common-mode filtering, voltage level translation using TXB0104D bidirectional shifters for interfacing Teensy 4.1 (3.3V) with 5V sensors and actuators, and power regulation using Traco DC-DC converters to step down the vehicle's 12V supply to the 5V and 3.3V rails required by the electronics. The schematic follows automotive-grade design practices with decoupling capacitors, ESD protection, and clearly defined power domains.

PCB Layout

The PCB layout optimizes signal integrity for the CAN bus differential pairs while maintaining clean power plane separation between the high-current motor driver connections and sensitive analog signals. Component placement prioritizes short trace lengths for high-speed signals and thermal management for the DC-DC converters. The board uses a multi-layer stackup to accommodate the routing density while keeping the form factor compact enough for the vehicle's electronics bay.

Power System

The power distribution system converts the vehicle's 12V battery supply into regulated 5V and 3.3V rails. Traco DC-DC converters provide high-efficiency step-down conversion with low output ripple. The design includes reverse polarity protection, overcurrent fuses, and power sequencing to ensure clean startup of all microcontroller nodes on the CAN bus. Separate power domains isolate the analog sensor circuits from digital switching noise.
